# Gen Z Outdoor Participation & Preferences

**Source:** Outdoor Industry Association Participation Report 2025, YPulse Gen Z Survey, TikTok Trends Data  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Demographic Overview

- **Gen Z definition:** Born 1997-2012 (ages 14-29 in 2026)
- **US Gen Z population:** 68M (20% of total population)
- **Outdoor participation rate:** 62% (highest of any generation)
- **Average annual outdoor spending:** $890 (vs. $1,240 Millennials, $980 Gen X)

## Post-Pandemic Outdoor Boom

### Participation Surge
- Gen Z outdoor participation up 28% since 2019
- Hiking: +45% (most accessible entry point)
- Camping: +35% (car camping dominant)
- Climbing: +52% (driven by gym-to-outdoor progression)
- Skiing/snowboarding: +18% (cost barrier limits growth)

### "Outdoor Adjacent" Activities
- Van life exploration (even without ownership): 34% interest
- Foraging/nature identification apps: 28% active users
- Outdoor photography/content creation: 56% motivation factor
- Wellness hiking (mental health focus): 71% cite as benefit

### Barriers to Entry
| Barrier | % Citing | Trend |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Cost of gear | 67% | Stable |
| Lack of knowledge | 54% | Declining (content helps) |
| No one to go with | 48% | Rising |
| Transportation | 41% | Stable |
| Don't feel welcome | 23% | Declining |

## Gorpcore Fashion Trend

### What Is Gorpcore?
Fashion trend where outdoor/technical wear becomes everyday style. "GORP" = Good Old Raisins and Peanuts (trail mix).

### Market Impact
- Arc'teryx revenue up 40% YoY (fashion crossover)
- Salomon shoes became streetwear staple
- The North Face Nuptse jacket: 2.3M units sold (2025)
- Gorpcore market value: $8.2B globally

### Gen Z Gorpcore Behaviors
- **74%** own outdoor gear they've never used outdoors
- **58%** bought outdoor brand for fashion, later went outdoors
- **42%** follow outdoor brands for aesthetic, not function
- **Cross-shopping:** 67% shop both Ssense and REI

### Implications
- Fashion entry point can convert to participation
- Style matters as much as function for Gen Z purchases
- "Ugly outdoor" aesthetic is actually desirable
- Collaboration with fashion brands expands reach

## Social Media Influence

### Platform Preferences
| Platform | Outdoor Content Engagement | Key Format |
|----------|---------------------------|------------|
| TikTok | 4.2B views (#hiking) | Short-form storytelling |
| YouTube | 12M outdoor subscribers (top 100 channels) | Long-form education |
| Instagram | Declining but still relevant | Aesthetic photography |
| Reddit | r/CampingGear 1.2M members | Reviews, Q&A |

### Content That Converts
- "What I actually bring" packing videos (highest conversion rate)
- Gear failures and honest reviews (trust building)
- Solo trip content (addresses "no one to go with" barrier)
- Budget gear roundups (cost barrier solution)
- "First time trying X" content (accessibility messaging)

### Influencer Landscape
- Micro-influencers (10K-100K) outperform macro for outdoor
- Authenticity > polish (Gen Z can detect sponsorship)
- Diverse representation drives engagement (up 3x when inclusive)

## Sustainability Expectations

### Non-Negotiables for Gen Z
- **89%** expect brands to have sustainability position
- **72%** research brand ethics before purchase
- **61%** willing to pay more for sustainable option
- **45%** have boycotted brand over environmental stance

### What "Sustainable" Means to Gen Z
1. Recycled materials (baseline expectation)
2. Repair programs (Patagonia Worn Wear model)
3. Carbon transparency (scope 1, 2, 3 disclosure)
4. Living wages in supply chain
5. End-of-life responsibility (take-back programs)

### Greenwashing Skepticism
- 78% don't trust brand sustainability claims without third-party certification
- Most trusted: B Corp, Bluesign, Fair Trade
- Least trusted: Self-reported carbon neutral claims
