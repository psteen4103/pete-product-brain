## Loyalty Program Comparison

| Feature | Summit Co-op Co-op | Alpine Trailwear | Ridgeline Outfitters | Contoso |
|---------|-----------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Model | Dividend (10%) | None | Points | Points |
| Experiential perks | Classes, rentals | Repairs, activism | Gearheads | None |
| Sustainability tie-in | Used gear program | Worn Wear, repairs | Limited | None |

**Opportunity:** Summit Co-op's experiential model drives exceptional retention. Tiered approach with experiences could differentiate. See [Loyalty v2 PRD](/specs/loyalty-program-v2.md).

## Key Differentiators by Competitor

### Summit Co-op
- Co-op membership creates emotional investment
- In-store experiences (classes, rentals) drive web traffic
- Strong community and advocacy position

### Alpine Trailwear
- Brand values drive purchase decisions
- Worn Wear resale builds sustainability credibility
- Premium pricing supported by quality perception

### Ridgeline Outfitters
- "Gearhead" expert chat differentiates service
- Curated assortment vs. endless selection
- Strong in technical categories

## Contoso Opportunities

1. **Speed to checkout:** Closing the UX gap is table stakes (Q1 priority)
2. **Experiential loyalty:** Tiered program with adventure perks is whitespace
3. **Sustainability angle:** Gear trade-in program underutilized vs. competitors
4. **Price-value position:** Fill gap between mass market and Summit Co-op premium
